Accessibility Research

According to the world bank, “One billion people, or 15% of the world’s population, experience some form of disability. (The World Bank)” Disabilities like visual impairments or physical impairments can affect the way a user interacts with your website. Permanently disabled people may need to use accessibility tools to aid them in navigating the web. Tools like screen readers, screen magnification software, speech input software, or head pointers allow people with disabilities to navigate the web (UC Berkeley). However, your website will function differently with these tools. For example: a screen reader can’t comprehend an image, so using the alt-text tag in html to tell the screen reader what the image is allows someone using that to know what is on the page. Many web-page designers overlook that their page might not be accessible for people who cannot use a mouse. Temporary disabilities like wrist sprains make it difficult to use a mouse but you can still use a keyboard.

Why is this important to your website? Well, do you want customers? Customers who have difficulty navigating your website, may decide to go with a competitor, or be discouraged from buying your product, or never return to your website. How do you want to be seen? Do you want to be seen as a business that is thoughtful and inclusive? Designing so that people with disabilities can use your website, shows that you care about them. You thought about their needs and you want them to interact with your site or your product. Do you want your website to be fast, come up high in search results? Having transcripts of audio on your website or having alternate text for images can make your site more searchable (Rush, et al.). This can also make your site easier to translate since text is easier to translate by a computer than an image or an audio recording. More people able to use the site, means more clicks, more traffic, more customers.

To recap, building a website to be more accessible to all people means more people can and will use your website.
